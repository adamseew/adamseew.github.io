Linear programming

Exercise 1

Consider a linear program in its standard form:

  1. define formali the linear program using its general minimization expression, and
  2. derive the KKT conditions for such a program.

Exercise 2

Consider the standard linear problem (this is the exercise on page 390 n. 13.5 in the textbook):

$$\min_{\mathbf{x}\in\mathbb{R}^n}{J(\mathbf{x})=\mathbf{c}^T\mathbf{x}\,\,\,\text{s.t.}\,\,\,A\mathbf{x}=\mathbf{b},\,\mathbf{x}\geq 0},$$

with \(\mathbf{c}\in\,\mathbb{R}^n,\,b\in\,\mathbb{R}^m\) and \(A\in\,\mathbb{R}^{m\times n}\):

  1. show that its dual is expressed (with \(\mathbf{\lambda}\in\,\mathbb{R}^m\)):

$$\min_{\mathbf{b}\in\mathbb{R}^m}{J(\mathbf{b})=\mathbf{b}^T\mathbf{\lambda}\,\,\,\text{s.t.}\,\,\,A^T\mathbf{\lambda}\leq \mathbf{c},\,\mathbf{\lambda}\geq 0}.$$

Exercise 3

Two process stages are involved in the production of gold of different carats (kt). Let's suppose a gold production factory in Denmark profits 40 dollars for 18kt per gram of gold, 30 dollars for 14kt, and 20 dollars for 10kt.

To make a gold lingot of 1 kg, the following time in each process stage is required:

  1. 1 kg of 18k gold requires 3 hours in first, 2 hours in second stage,
  2. 1 kg of 14k gold requires 2 hours in first, 2 hours in second stage, and
  3. 1 kg of 10k gold requires 1 hour in first, 3 hours in second stage.
During 1 year, first stage has 7 200 hours and second stage had 6 000 hours of available production time. The rest is needed due to the maintance of the production line. The available time should be fully utilized.

We wish to maximize the yearly profit.

  1. define the LP problem,
  2. use some of the KKT condition to identify the points of the optimization problem that might be optimal, and
  3. show which of the points found at the previous point respect all the KKT condition.
