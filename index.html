About me

I like writing; in particular, algorithms to make mobile robots move and fly. My main research interests lie at the intersection of robotics, computer science, and optimal control. I work with planning algorithms for various autonomous mobile robotics use cases.

Currently, I am a Postdoc at the GRAB Lab at Yale in the U.S., where I am working on different techniques surveying biodiversity using mobile ground-based and aerial robots. I got my Ph.D. in Robotics in 2022 from the University of Southern Denmark, where I worked with professor Ulrik Pagh Schultz at the Unmanned Aerial Systems Center. I developed simultaneous path planning and task scheduling for the Optrerra drone, an aerial robot employed for precision agriculture. I have been researching different techniques for this, including optimal state estimation, data-driven control, and MPC.

I was previously with the Altair Robotics Lab at the University of Verona in Italy, where I earned my M.Sc. in Computer Science in 2018, advised by professor Paolo Fiorini. My thesis examined indirect methods for trajectory optimization with intermediate constraints for quadrotors.

Personal

I grew up swimming and programming in western Slovakia. Specifically, between Bratislava and Sasinkovo, a tiny winemakers' village situated a little less than an hour drive from the capital where I spent most of my summers. I am a space exploration enthusiast, and in my free time, I enjoy outdoor activities such as open water swimming, hiking, cycling, and running.
